## 1. Engagement Fundamentals
*Status: Researched (initial pass) — 2026-07-21*

**Do:**
- Cap committed bandwidth — don't let one engagement consume more than
  ~60% of capacity; keep room for a second client and business-dev.
- Insist on a real scope, not a title. "Help with growth" isn't a scope —
  push for specific outcomes, measurable goals, a timeframe. Vague
  scoping is the most-cited first-engagement failure mode.
- Insist on real authority (sign-off on OKRs/roadmap), not just advisory
  access — fits his stated differentiator (execution over
  strategy-writing); "advice only" engagements set him up to be blamed
  for outcomes he can't actually drive.
- Build in a coaching/succession mandate — fractional engagements often
  include growing the eventual full-time head of product; reduces client
  risk and is worth naming explicitly in the pitch.
- Design deliberate visibility/trust-building early (intros, structured
  presence) rather than being "hidden" from the org — integration
  failure is the most-cited reason engagements underperform.
- Japan-specific: decision cycles run long — the differentiator is
  speed/precision *within* that cycle, not fighting it (per a cited
  Tokyo interim-CFO case).

**Don't:**
- Don't accept engagements with vague scope or advisory-only authority.
- Don't skip explicit onboarding/authority setup assuming it'll "sort
  itself out" — it doesn't.
- Don't let a client treat part-time as "less committed" — stay visibly
  accountable, not "just temporary."
- Avoid reactive feature/priority churn without a testing plan —
  undermines the "operator with judgment" positioning.

**Rate context found:** fractional VP of Product roles commonly run
$5,000–12,000/month or $100–250/hr in current market data — roughly in
line with the ¥100,000–150,000/day starting rate (FACTS.md), possibly
conservative depending on assumed hours/scope. Not Japan-specific pricing
— worth a dedicated pass (see To Do).
