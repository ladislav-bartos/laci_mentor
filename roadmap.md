**Prepared July 2026 · Living document — we revise as we learn what works**

## The Strategy in One Paragraph

You have until March 2027 on full salary with permission to contract (but not full-time employment). Your Japanese speaking is currently the weakest link, but English-market contracting doesn't need it. So: earn and build credibility in English **now**, rebuild spoken Japanese and pass N2 **in parallel**, and enter 2027 with (a) a running contracting business, (b) functional business Japanese, and (c) a warm network — giving you three viable paths to ¥40M+: scaled contracting, gaishikei full-time, or a hybrid.

**Why this order:** Waiting until your Japanese is "good enough" wastes the double-income window and delays proof that your company works. Contracting in English first de-risks everything.

## Phase 0 — Foundation (July, ~2 weeks)

1. **Client exclusion rule (decided):** No HR-tech, job-board, or recruitment-platform clients until April 2027. Simple, safe, removes ambiguity. *Reasoning: your remaining garden leave salary is not worth risking, and your other target markets are large enough.*
2. **Get your company operationally ready:** accountant/tax advisor confirmed, invoicing template, standard contract template (業務委託契約), business bank account active, decide your day rate. Suggested starting rate: ¥100,000–150,000/day for overseas clients (≈ $650–1,000), based on 20 yrs senior PM + GCP. *Reasoning: at ¥120k/day, ~15 billable days/month ≈ ¥21M/yr — a realistic 2027 baseline that stacks toward ¥40M with rate increases or retainers.*
   - **Dual-entity advantage:** you also have a UK company. UK/EU/US clients often prefer contracting with a UK Ltd (familiar law, GBP/USD invoicing, no Japanese paperwork). Ask your accountants **which entity should invoice which clients** — this is a tax question (permanent establishment, where the work is performed) that needs professional advice, but having both entities is a genuine sales asset.
3. **Define your offer in one sentence.** Recommended positioning: *"Fractional/interim senior product leader for companies entering or scaling in Japan, with hands-on GCP and AI-automation capability."* The Japan angle is your moat — few senior PMs have 10+ years on the ground here. *Reasoning: generic "senior PM contractor" competes globally on price; "Japan market + product + cloud" is a rare combination that commands premium rates.*
4. **Refresh LinkedIn** to match that positioning. Headline, about section, "open to consulting."

**Japanese**
1. Book a speaking tutor (italki/Preply) 3–4×/week, 30 min. Instruction: conversation only, no textbook. *Reasoning: your knowledge (N3) exceeds your output ability — you need retrieval practice, not more input.*
2. Start N2 grammar/vocab system: Bunpro or Anki deck, 30 min/day. Register for the **December JLPT in late August–early September** (registration closes early — this is a hard deadline).
3. Passive immersion on "dead time": Japanese podcasts during commute/chores.

## Phase 2 — Deepen + N2 (October–December)

1. **Deliver excellently on contract #1** — your first Japan-based reference client matters more than the revenue.
2. Attend networking events: **minimum 2/month, target 1/week** — daytime or evening, whichever fits. Mechanism: every Friday during sprint planning, review the next 2 weeks of events *and* coordinate evenings with your wife's schedule before committing. *Reasoning: Japan business runs on trust built face-to-face; the Friday two-week lookahead prevents the "no acceptable evening left" problem by booking family logistics before events fill your calendar. Daytime events (lunch meetups, coworking events, conference days) are your safety valve when evenings are taken.*
3. From November: shift Japanese study weight toward N2 mock exams (past papers, timed).
4. **Take JLPT N2 in December.** Honest note: N3→N2 in 5 months at 1 hr/day while rusty is *ambitious but possible* — roughly a 50/50 shot. If you miss it, July 2027 sitting is the backup and nothing else in the plan breaks. *Reasoning: I'd rather you know the odds than be blindsided.*
5. December: pipeline review — do you have contract #2 or a renewal lined up for Q1 2027?

## Phase 3 — The Fork (January–March 2027)

Garden leave ends March 2027. By January you'll have real data: contracting income run-rate, Japanese progress, network strength. Then choose:

- **Contracting is working (≥¥2.5M/month run-rate):** double down, raise rates, add a retainer client.
- **Contracting is slow:** start gaishikei interviews in January (hiring cycles mean Jan–Feb interviews → April starts). Your Indeed comp history anchors ¥35–45M offers at big tech / gaishikei.
- **Hybrid:** many people do 3–4 days contracting + build a product with the rest.

*Reasoning: we don't pick now because we'd be guessing. The whole point of 2026 is to generate the evidence.*

## Operating Rhythm: Sprints + Daily Standups (with Claude)

We run this like a product: **1-week sprints, Monday–Friday.**

**Daily standup (~10 min, start of your focus block):**
1. Yesterday: what got done / didn't
2. Today: top 1–3 tasks (from the sprint backlog)
3. Blockers + one observation: what's working, what isn't

**Friday sprint review + planning (~20 min):**
1. Score the week: tasks done, Japanese minutes logged, outreach sent, replies received, events attended
2. What worked → keep; what didn't → change or drop (the plan is a hypothesis, not scripture)
3. Plan next week's backlog, including: holidays, family commitments, wife's evening schedule → lock in any networking event now
4. Monthly (last Friday): check progress against phase goals and this roadmap; update the roadmap itself

**How to run it with me:** start each standup by telling me what happened yesterday; I'll track against the sprint, flag drift, and suggest adjustments. Honest reporting beats optimistic reporting — I can only improve the plan with real data, and "I did nothing yesterday" is useful information, not failure.

*Reasoning: weekly sprints match your explicit request, and they suit this plan because feedback loops here are short (outreach replies in days, study progress in weeks). A monthly cadence would let a failing tactic run 4x too long.*
