# Adhoc session — Positioning statement & LinkedIn refresh (2026-07-14)

Extended continuation of the Day 2 standup, covering the "positioning
statement" and "refresh LinkedIn" tasks in full.

## Starting point

User's earlier draft (from strategy chat, in FACTS.md): "Fractional/interim
senior product leader for companies entering or scaling in Japan, with
hands-on GCP and AI-automation capability." Skeleton was solid (role +
audience + differentiator) but missing an outcome/proof point.

## LinkedIn data gathered

- Fetched public LinkedIn headline/About (user confirmed accurate): "Technical
  Product Manager | AI Solutions Architect | ML Engineer | MBA | Quantum
  Computing" — generalist technical framing, not Japan/APAC-specific.
- User supplied two CV versions: v1 (technical/ML-engineering framing) and v2
  (VP of Product / Growth & Demand Generation / Global Expansion framing).
  These represented genuinely different personal brands aimed at different
  buyers (CTO/Head of Eng vs. CEO/CMO).

## Key decision: which direction to lead with

User asked directly: which gives better odds of landing contracting work at
target rate (¥100–150k/day, ¥40M+/year)? Recommendation: lead with the
growth/executive angle, not the technical-PM angle, because:
- "AI/ML Product Manager" is now a commoditized, oversupplied positioning
- Fractional VP-level work is priced as executive/strategic, not senior-IC
- "Global expansion into/from Japan" backed by a real held VP title (DAZN)
  and an MBA specifically in that strategy is a narrow, hard-to-copy niche
- Risk: no track record yet as an independent fractional operator — mitigated
  by leaning on the fact the VP title was substantive, not aspirational

## Iterations on the one-liner and headline

1. Added Japan + APAC framing (not Japan-only) — user is Japan-based but has
   DAZN Japan & APAC experience; western companies expand regionally, not
   just into one country.
2. Added AI-adoption angle for product teams — a real 2026 pain point (what
   to automate with AI vs. not), positioned as mentoring at first, later
   revised to execution-focused per user's stated strength ("everyone can
   write a GTM strategy, few can execute and adapt it").
3. Dropped "for Western Companies" from the headline — real headline examples
   specify audience by *stage*, not client nationality; also unnecessarily
   excludes non-Western companies expanding into Japan.
4. Replaced "launch and scale" with "expand" — "launch" reads as
   early-stage/0-to-1, which conflicted with the user's stated target
   audience (growth-stage to enterprise, not early-stage startups).
5. Replaced "executing market-entry roadmaps" with more concrete,
   outcome-oriented language ("turning expansion plans into shipped
   results").
6. Explored "Fractional VP of Product" vs. "Fractional CPO" — researched
   market demand; no clean data on relative volume, but recommended VP of
   Product because CPO is genuinely ambiguous (Product/People/
   Procurement/Process) and VP of Product better matches the
   execution-over-vision differentiator.
7. Final refinement: introduced "Growth" (business growth — acquisition,
   retention, revenue) and "Transformation" (team/process/OKR improvement,
   AI-driven internal efficiency) as the two explicit focus pillars,
   replacing vaguer "expansion" and "AI adoption" language throughout.
   User asked this be captured as a stable fact and used to frame future
   proposals — done, see FACTS.md Goals section.

## About section — development

Researched LinkedIn About best practices: first person, under 300 words,
value-centered (not credential-listing), each role as a mini case study
(context → action → quantified result), explicit capacity/CTA closing line.

Found and reviewed 7 real fractional/interim Head of Product / VP of Product
/ CPO job postings (Sortly, a social eCommerce marketplace, a Salesforce-based
SaaS tool, a cosmetic-procedures ads marketplace, Token [cybersecurity CPO],
Share Equity [health-tech nonprofit CPO]) plus 2 composite archetypes
(streaming/subscription, international market-entry) built from aggregate
research since no live postings existed for those categories. Mapped each to
the user's real experience and asked for real metrics rather than inventing
placeholder numbers.

User supplied real metrics:
- DAZN: 0-to-1 Japan launch, 1M+ subscribers within 12 months, core team of 4
- Rakuten: led team of 70+ (DevOps/FE/BE/mobile/PM/QA), mobile app migration
  → +20% retention, +30% conversion
- Indeed: improved job-matching/ranking algorithm performance by 25%

Iterated wording per user feedback: MBA folded into flowing prose (not a
parenthetical); "roadmap for personalisation..." replaced with "growth,
acquisition, and retention OKRs"; GCP-certifications clause and
bilingual-language claim removed (Japanese not yet business-level); CTA
changed from "Japan and APAC expansion" to "Japan and APAC growth or
transformation."

## Final gut-check: JD fit and pros/cons

Reviewed the final wording against the 7 real postings + 2 composites:
- **Strong fit:** Social eCommerce Marketplace (0-to-1 launch), and both
  composite archetypes (streaming/subscription, market-entry) — these are
  the strongest, most differentiated lane
- **Moderate fit:** Sortly and the Salesforce SaaS tool — revenue-growth and
  AI-integration boxes checked, but both are startup-scale ($15–25M revenue,
  2-PM teams) vs. the user's large-org proof points (70-person teams, 1M
  subscribers) — a real scale mismatch risk for smaller companies
- **Weak fit:** cosmetic-ads marketplace (no named BI tools/ad-tech domain),
  Token (no cybersecurity domain), Share Equity (no healthcare domain)

**Pros:** rare quantified Japan market-entry proof; big-name credibility
(DAZN/Rakuten/Indeed); results at three separate companies; broad
cross-functional span; Growth+Transformation covers two distinct buyer
triggers; genuine technical fluency.

**Cons:** scale mismatch for smaller/leaner startups; no B2B SaaS domain
history (all consumer subscription/marketplace/hiring-platform); technical
credibility now asserted rather than certification-backed (GCP certs line
was cut per user request); no named BI tools before this session's fix;
Japanese not yet business-level, unaddressed in the profile.

**Decision:** keep the current wording — it deliberately leans into
large-company/enterprise proof rather than diluting it to also appeal to
early-stage startups. User confirmed this final version.
