# EVENTS.md — Networking Events Tracker (living doc)

Purpose: track concrete, recurring networking events/orgs worth attending,
their cadence, and why they fit the Ideal Contact Profile (see `FACTS.md`)
— so picking "2-3 candidate events" each sprint is a lookup, not a fresh
search every time. Complements `SOURCES.md` (online contact-sourcing
channels) — this file is about events to physically/virtually attend.

## How to use this file
- Check the "Where to check dates" URL for each candidate before
  committing — cadence here is typical pattern, not a live calendar.
- Cross-check against `SPRINT.md`'s scheduling-constraints section
  (family/wife's schedule) before booking anything, per CLAUDE.md.
- Weekends are never in scope — evening events only work on nights without
  a family commitment; day events need to fit inside the 2-3 hr/weekday
  work capacity.
- "ICP fit" flags whether the typical audience actually includes his
  target titles (CEO/COO/CPO, Head/VP Product, Country Manager Japan,
  CHRO/Head of Talent) — events skewed to early-stage founders or IC-level
  peers are noted as lower priority, not removed, since peer intel /
  volume still has some value.
- "Category" tags what kind of opportunity it is (see below) — added
  2026-07-17 since attend-only, in-person-Tokyo was too narrow a lens.

## Categories (added 2026-07-17 — broadened scope, not just in-person Tokyo attendance)
- **Chamber/Business** — foreign chamber of commerce or business-council
  networking (the original core of this list)
- **PM-peer** — product-management community, mostly IC/mid-level
- **Startup-adjacent** — startup ecosystem events (demo days, founder
  meetups, hackathons/competitions). Not the ICP directly, but useful for:
  (a) investors and larger companies scouting talent also show up, (b)
  staying current on what's new — useful both for personal growth and as
  outreach/LinkedIn content ("what I'm seeing in the startup ecosystem")
- **Online/Virtual** — not restricted to physically being in Tokyo;
  in-person Tokyo is still preferred when a choice exists, but online
  expands volume without evening/family-schedule conflicts
- **Speaking opportunity** — events where he could be a speaker (main or
  secondary) rather than just attend, e.g. on applying AI for
  growth/efficiency — higher-leverage than attending, since it builds
  visible credibility with an audience rather than one relationship at a
  time. Currently no CFP/speaker-search research has been done — this is
  a gap, see To Do below
- **Tangential/creative** — events not directly about interim/fractional
  hiring or his exact ICP, from adjacent domains, chosen deliberately for
  what they might teach or who might unexpectedly be in the room. Also a
  research gap — see To Do below
