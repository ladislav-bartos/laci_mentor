# CONTENT.md — LinkedIn/Content Strategy (living doc)

Purpose: a working content system for his fractional/interim VP of
Product positioning — a backlog of specific posts to write, the
higher-level topic categories that generate more of them over time, a
watchlist of people posting in this space, a ranked library of their
best posts for inspiration, and the best-practice guidance behind it
all. Complements `CONTRACTING.md` (which covers the *strategy* of
marketing himself as a fractional exec, section 2) — this file is the
day-to-day *content* engine that feeds off that strategy.

## How to use this file
- Section 1 is the actionable backlog — pull from here when it's time to
  write a post.
- Section 2 is the idea generator — when the 10-post backlog runs low,
  come back here and derive new specific posts from these categories.
- Sections 3-4 are for inspiration when stuck — who to look at, and
  their actual best posts, ranked.
- Section 5 defines how this file stays current — see that section for
  the weekly-check mechanism and its current status.
- Section 6 is for expanding beyond LinkedIn once that channel is
  proven.
- Section 7 is the reference to check tone/approach against before
  publishing anything.

## 2. High-level topics (the idea engine)
*Use these to generate the next batch once the list above runs low.
Each maps to at least one of the 10 above and to real examples in §4.*

- **Case Study** — a specific past engagement/project with a quantified
  before/after result. Confirmed as a rewarded category (real examples
  found: $5MM profit-increase quarter, 30% retention uplift, "drove 4X
  product usage"). Maps to #1, #3, #7. *Future prompts:* any other
  quantified win from his career not yet covered (Amazon, Okomp?).
- **Framework / Mental Model** — an original categorization or decision
  tool (e.g. a 2x2 matrix). The single best-performing format found
  (Aakash Gupta, 322 reactions/56 comments). Maps to #2. *Future
  prompts:* a framework for "how to scope a fractional Product
  engagement," or "stages of a Japan market-entry product launch."
- **Debate / Contrarian Take** — a genuinely contestable, defensible
  claim. Drives the most comments of any category found (Mackey, 67
  comments; Gupta, 56). Maps to #4. *Future prompts:* a take on whether
  AI reduces or increases the need for senior product judgment; whether
  "product-led growth" is overused as advice for enterprise clients.
- **Market/Cultural Observation** — a specific, embedded-in-the-space
  insight (not generic trend commentary). Maps to #5. *Future prompts:*
  observations on Japan's long decision cycles, or on how growth-stage
  companies actually structure product teams when it expanding into
  APAC.
- **Positioning/Stage-Fit Explainer** — "why X works for stage/context
  Y." Mirrors Schneider's 166/45 performer. Maps to #6. *Future
  prompts:* "why growth-stage-to-enterprise clients need different
  things from a fractional VP of Product than a Series A startup does."
- **Personal Journey / Vulnerability** — honestly answering the
  questions people are already asking privately. Maps to #8. *Future
  prompts:* what surprised him most in his first month of fractional
  work (once he has one); how he decided ¥100-150k/day as a starting
  rate.
- **Value-First Teaser** — a specific, narrow preview of a productized
  offer, not a pitch. Maps to #9. *Future prompts:* a specific
  before/after from an actual AI-maturity mini-session, once a few have
  been run.
- **Reflective/Philosophy** — lower-stakes musing on what the fractional
  role itself is/isn't. Maps to #10. *Future prompts:* what "operator
  judgment" actually means in practice; the DevOps/AI-automation habits
  he'd bring into any org regardless of client.
- **Trend/Data Commentary** *(use carefully)* — reacting to a market
  stat. Found to reliably *underperform* unless paired with a personal
  story or specific claim (Maxfield's stat-only post got only 7
  reactions) — never post a stat alone; always attach his own take or a
  concrete example.

## 5. Weekly content monitoring
*Status: designed 2026-07-21, not yet actually running on a schedule —
see the open question below.*

**What a weekly check should do:**
1. For each person in §3's watchlist, check for new posts since the
   last check.
2. Add any new post that's clearly on-topic (fractional/interim
   product-leadership content) to §4's library, ranked by engagement.
3. Check for new best-practice articles (LinkedIn strategy for
   executives, fractional-content guidance) — fold genuinely new
   findings into §7.
4. Note the check date at the top of this section so staleness is
   visible at a glance.

**Open question — how should "automatic" actually run?** Two options,
meaningfully different in commitment level:
- **(a) On-demand command**, run manually — e.g. at each Friday review,
  mirroring how `/research-events` etc. already work. Nothing runs
  without him or a session explicitly triggering it.
- **(b) True unattended automation** — a scheduled cloud agent (cron)
  that runs weekly on its own, no session needed to trigger it. This is
  a meaningfully bigger commitment than anything built so far in this
  repo (everything else is conversational/on-demand) — it would run and
  commit to this repo unattended. Needs explicit setup via the
  `schedule` skill, and worth confirming he actually wants unattended
  automation vs. a manual weekly command, before setting it up.

*Not yet built either way — flagging for his decision rather than
picking one.*

## 7. Best practices — do/don't, tone, examples
*Consolidated from CONTRACTING.md §2's research (full sourcing there)
plus the ranked library in §4 above, which doubles as good/bad examples.*

**Do:**
- Post specific dialogue/story or an original framework — the two
  formats that dominate the top of §4's ranking.
- Quantify everything — case studies need real before/after numbers,
  not "significant improvement."
- Write something genuinely contestable when doing a debate-style post
  — safe consensus statements draw far fewer comments (compare rank 1-2
  vs. rank 8 in §4).
- Keep posting cadence to 3-5x/week including ~1 video/week once a
  rhythm is established (see CONTRACTING.md §2) — not required from
  day one.
- Tone: first-person, direct, willing to state a real opinion — every
  high performer in §4 has a clear point of view, not a hedge.

**Don't:**
- Don't post a generic pros/cons checklist — this is the *lowest*-
  performing format found (§4, rank 12).
- Don't reshare someone else's guide expecting their engagement to
  transfer — it doesn't (§4, rank 10 vs. rank 4, same underlying guide).
- Don't lead with a market stat alone — pair it with a personal story or
  specific claim, or skip it (§4, rank 11).
- Don't make availability/"open to work" the subject of a post — keep
  that in the profile, not the content (CONTRACTING.md §2).

**Good examples to study (see §4 for links):** rank 1 (Mackey — story),
rank 2 (Gupta — framework), rank 3 (Schneider — stage-fit explainer).

**Weaker examples to learn from, not copy (see §4 for links):** rank
10-12 (Hensley x2, Maxfield) — all three are generically-formatted or
un-personalized despite covering reasonable topics, which is precisely
why format/specificity matters more than topic choice alone.

he gains real posting experience — e.g. what actually worked for his
own audience vs. what the research predicted.*
